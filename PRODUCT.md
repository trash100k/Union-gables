# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Affluent, design-literate travelers who are "over being marketed to." Four segments, two primary:

- **The racing set** (primary) — owners, breeders, lifelong meet-goers, moneyed regulars. Want proximity to the Saratoga Race Course gate, discretion, a *house* not a lobby, and a great table at night. Peak: the meet, mid-July–Labor Day.
- **Romance / anniversary couples** (primary) — want fireplaces, the porch, the gardens, dinner they don't have to leave for. Year-round, strong off-season.
- **Arts & culture** (secondary) — SPAC, Skidmore, Yaddo, museum visitors wanting a refined, walkable base.
- **Wellness / weddings** (secondary) — "take the waters" calm off-season; the house + grounds + supper club as a private venue.

Context: booking a stay for a season, not a weekend. They respond to restraint, provenance, and confidence — not urgency banners or "BOOK NOW."

## Product Purpose

Market the Union Gables Inn — a c.1901 Queen Anne Victorian house at 55 Union Avenue, a block and a half from Saratoga Race Course, with the Libby Supper Club inside. The site's job is not to advertise a hotel but to make a particular guest "feel that they've already been let in." Success: a first-time visitor understands within one screen that this is *a trackside Victorian house with a supper club*, not a downtown hotel; the site reads calm and confident; off-season pages give non-racing guests a real reason to come; the "living, breathing" motion is felt without costing legibility or speed.

## Brand Personality

Confident, dry, a little literary. Old money, kept quiet. Never pleads. Reference register: "The porch has been waiting since 1901." / "The season is short. The porch is not." Emotional goal: the calm, private, been-let-in feeling of slipping back to the house after the party. Strategic line: *The Adelphi is the party. Union Gables is the house you slip back to after it.*

## Anti-references

- **The Adelphi / Saratoga Arms / grand downtown hotels** — do not compete on "grand" and "luxurious" spectacle or Gatsby glamour. Out-*calm* them, don't out-shout them.
- **Urgency marketing** — no countdown clocks, no "BOOK NOW," no scarcity banners. Scarcity during the meet is real; state it once, with a straight face.
- **Generic hotel framing** — no "hotel room" stock photography, no stock smiles, no marble-lobby energy.
- **Racing-superlative inflation** — do not write "oldest racetrack in America" (it isn't); accuracy is a quiet flex.

## Design Principles

1. **Geography is the brand.** Lead with place and rhythm (Union Avenue, the meet, the seasons); let room inventory follow.
2. **Out-calm, don't out-shout.** Restraint and confidence are the differentiator against grand-hotel competitors.
3. **A house, not a hotel.** Intimate, residential, provenance-forward — real turrets, real antiques, an acre of private grounds.
4. **Living and breathing, never bouncing.** Motion should drift and settle like dust in sun or the field after a race — and never cost legibility or speed.
5. **Accuracy as a quiet flex.** Anchor copy to verified facts; let competitors fudge the superlatives.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

The "living, breathing" motion must never cost legibility or speed: honor `prefers-reduced-motion`, lazy-load photography, keep LCP fast. Small gold labels are brightened (`--gold-hi`) for AA contrast on gradient surfaces; keyboard focus is visible on all interactive elements; a skip link is present. Target: WCAG AA for body and large text against the deep-green ground.
