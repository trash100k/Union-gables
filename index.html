I The Mansion

Old Saratoga,
kept exactly.

Built at the turn of the century, when the racing crowd came north by private rail and the town wore its money quietly. Three turrets, a wraparound porch, and rooms dressed in the period antiques they were furnished with — nothing reproduced, nothing rushed.

Beyond the parlor: better than an acre of perennial gardens, a quiet run of fountains, and a seasonal Romanesque pool that asks only that you do very little. Mornings arrive as a full breakfast, cooked to order. After­noons as something just out of the oven.

  • 1901 Queen Anne Victorian, on the National Register
  • 1.5 blocks to the Saratoga Race Course
  • 125+ restaurants, stages & galleries on foot
  • 1 porch, and all the time you need on it
II The Rooms

A handful of rooms,
each with a name.

Oversized chambers and suites, every one with its own bath, a natural-gas fireplace, and antiques chosen for the room rather than the catalogue.

A

The Annie

The turret room on the west side. Tall windows on three sides, a king under luxury linen, marble-topped tables and wall-to-wall orientals. The light arrives early and stays late.

King · Turret · Fireplace
K

The Kate

A corner suite with a sitting nook and a clawfoot soak. Deep greens, a quiet fireplace, and the garden two storeys down. The one guests ask for by name a year out.

C

The Cindy

Intimate and warm, with a four-poster and a reading chair set to the afternoon sun. Closest to the porch, and the smell of baking that drifts up around four o'clock.

III Libby Supper Club

Six courses.
One seating.

Dinner kept the old way — a single prix fixe that turns over with the month and bends to whatever the regional growers send. New American at heart, with an ear for French, Italian, and plain rustic cooking. Pescatarian and vegetarian nights are set aside each month.
