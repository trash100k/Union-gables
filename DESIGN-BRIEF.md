# Union Gables — Design Brief

*A brand and web-design direction for the Union Gables Inn site, grounded in deep
research on Saratoga Springs, NY. Prepared June 2026.*

---

## 0. The one-line insight

> Every other grand name in Saratoga sells you **downtown Broadway and Gilded-Age
> spectacle.** Union Gables sells you the **quiet side of the same money** — a private
> 1901 Victorian house on the road to the track, where the only thing on a schedule is
> the first race. Don't out-shout the Adelphi. Out-*calm* it.

The site's job is not to advertise a hotel. It is to make a particular kind of guest feel
that they've already been let in.

---

## 1. The place (only the parts that change design decisions)

Saratoga Springs markets itself, verbatim, as **"Health, History, Horses and the Arts"** —
the original "Health, History, Horses" with "and the Arts" appended after SPAC opened.[^1]
Four pillars, and Union Gables sits at the literal crossing of the strongest two.

- **Horses.** Saratoga Race Course is **on Union Avenue**, organized by John Morrissey in
  **1863**, with a 40-day summer meet running mid-July to Labor Day, climaxing in the
  Travers (first run 1864).[^2][^3] **Union Gables is on that same avenue** — a block and a
  half from the gate. This is the property's single most ownable fact.
- **Health.** The naturally **carbonated mineral springs** made Saratoga the *"Queen of the
  Spas"*; people came to "take the waters." The tradition lives on at the Roosevelt Baths
  in **Saratoga Spa State Park** (2,379 acres, year-round mineral baths).[^4][^5]
- **Arts.** **SPAC** (opened 1966, ~500,000 visitors a year, summer homes of New York City
  Ballet and the Philadelphia Orchestra) is the off-track cultural engine and a key
  shoulder-season demand driver.[^6]
- **The rhythm.** Demand and rates are **sharply seasonal**, peaking across the ~8–9 week
  meet. The town's year-round population is only **~28,500** but swells enormously in
  summer.[^7] The site must work for *two different cities*: meet-season Saratoga (sold out,
  electric, premium) and the quiet off-season (springs, foliage, romance, holidays).

**Design consequence:** the site should breathe with the *seasons of the meet*, not the
hours of a booking funnel. Lead with place and rhythm; let the room inventory follow.

---

## 2. Where Union Gables actually stands

- **Address as positioning.** 55 Union Avenue. Not "near downtown" — *on the racing
  corridor*, inside the historic district, among the Victorian mansions the racing money
  built. Geography is the brand.
- **The house.** A c.1901 Queen Anne Victorian — turrets, wraparound porch, period
  antiques, an acre of gardens, fountains, a seasonal Romanesque pool. (Local lore: built
  by merchant George Crippen, nicknamed *"Sunnyside."* Single-source — verify before
  putting in copy.)[^8]
- **The table.** The **Libby Supper Club** inside the mansion — a six-course, monthly prix
  fixe — is a genuine differentiator. None of the competitive set pairs an intimate
  Victorian inn with a destination supper club. **Lean on this hard.**

---

## 3. Who we're designing for

| Segment | What they want | When | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The racing set** — owners, breeders, lifelong meet-goers, the moneyed regulars | Proximity to the gate, discretion, a *house* not a lobby, a great table at night | The meet (Jul–Sep) | **Primary** |
| **Romance / anniversary couples** | Fireplaces, the porch, the garden, dinner you don't have to leave for | Year-round; strong off-season | **Primary** |
| **Arts & culture (SPAC, museums, Yaddo, Skidmore visitors)** | Walkable elegance, a refined base, late dinner after a concert | Summer + shoulder | Secondary |
| **Wellness / "take the waters"** | Calm, gardens, the springs nearby, slowness | Off-season, midweek | Secondary |
| **Weddings & small events** | The house, the grounds, the supper club, exclusivity | Spring–fall | Secondary (high value) |

The unifying guest is **affluent, design-literate, and over being marketed to** (median
household income in town is ~$100k; the meet draws far wealthier).[^7] They respond to
restraint, provenance, and confidence — not urgency banners and "BOOK NOW."

---

## 4. The competitive set — and the open lane

| Property | Position | Where it lives |
|---|---|---|
| **The Adelphi** (1877) | "Saratoga's premier luxury hotel," Victorian-meets-modern, *Great Gatsby* glamour[^9] | **Downtown, 365 Broadway** |
| **Saratoga Arms** | Luxury boutique, "historic elegance meets modern luxury"[^10] | **Downtown, 497 Broadway** |
| **Batcheller Mansion Inn** (1873) | High-Victorian-Gothic icon, the photogenic mansion-inn[^11] | **Congress Park / downtown** |
| **The Gideon Putnam** | Classic grand resort *inside the Spa State Park*, spa-anchored | **Spa park** |
| **Union Gables** | Intimate 1901 Victorian *house* + supper club | **Union Avenue — trackside** |

**The gap is obvious.** The prestige competitors cluster on **Broadway/downtown** and
compete on *grand* and *luxurious*. Two positions are unclaimed and Union Gables already
owns the deeds to both:

1. **Trackside / on Union Avenue** — closest in feeling to the racing itself.
2. **A private house, not a hotel** — quiet old money vs. grand-hotel spectacle; an acre of
   garden and your own porch vs. a marble lobby.

**Strategic line:** *The Adelphi is the party. Union Gables is the house you slip back to
after it.*

---

## 5. Brand positioning

**Positioning statement**
> For the people who come to Saratoga for the season — not the weekend — Union Gables is
> the private 1901 Victorian on Union Avenue, a block and a half from the first race and a
> staircase from the best table in town. Old Saratoga, kept exactly, and kept quiet.

**Differentiators to repeat everywhere**
1. **On Union Avenue** — the racing corridor, not downtown.
2. **A house, not a hotel** — intimate, residential, an acre of private grounds.
3. **The Libby Supper Club** — six courses without leaving the porch.
4. **1901, intact** — real antiques, real turrets, real provenance.

**Voice:** confident, dry, a little literary. Never pleads. The current site copy
("*The porch has been waiting since 1901*" / "*The season is short. The porch is not.*")
is the right register — extend it, don't dilute it.

## 6. Art direction

The existing build (deep racing-green + antique gold, Cormorant Garamond, the breathing
Three.js gilded-dust hero) is **on-strategy** — refine, don't restart.

- **Palette.** Keep racing/turf green + brass/antique gold + ivory as the spine. Introduce
  **one disciplined accent of racing-silk color** (oxblood is already in; a deep cherry or
  royal could appear sparingly in the racing/Libby contexts) — a nod to jockey silks, used
  like a ribbon, never a theme.
- **Type.** Cormorant/EB Garamond is correct for "1901 old money." Hold the line on the
  letter-spaced sans for labels. Add a true **engraved/letterpress** texture cue for
  section numerals and the monogram.
- **Motion = "living, breathing."** The brief's headline word. The hero particles, the
  scroll reveals, the parallax already deliver it. Extend the metaphor: things should
  *drift and settle*, like dust in sun or the field after a race — never bounce or pop.
- **Photography.** Now that the real CDN photography is wired in, direct future shoots
  toward: **the porch at golden hour, the gardens, the turrets against sky, the supper-club
  table candlelit, a single brass detail.** Avoid generic "hotel room" framing. People,
  rarely and tastefully (a hand, a glass, never a stock smile).
- **The two seasons.** Consider a subtle seasonal state: a warmer, brighter, busier feel
  for *meet season*; a quieter, foggier, fireside feel off-season. Even a single swapped
  hero line ("*The meet is on.*" vs. "*The town is ours again.*") earns its keep.

## 7. Site architecture

```
Home (the living hero, the confident invitation)
├── The Mansion        — history, the house, the grounds, the porch
├── The Rooms          — all named rooms/suites; filterable; fireplace/turret/pet tags
├── Libby Supper Club  — the six-course story, the room, reservations (dine@)
├── The Season         — NEW: the meet, the Travers, SPAC, the springs; "why you came"
├── Weddings & Gatherings — NEW: the house + grounds + supper club as a private venue
├── The Grounds        — gardens, pool, fountains
├── Reserve            — dates, direct-booking, the quiet close
└── Journal (optional) — slow content: a race remembered, a spring, a recipe from Libby
```

**Booking strategy.** Keep the *tone* unhurried but make the *path* frictionless: one
persistent, understated "Reserve" affordance; real availability behind it. Off-season,
surface **packages** (springs/wellness, romance, holiday, SPAC-night dinner-and-stay) to
fight the seasonality. During the meet, scarcity is real — state it once, with a straight
face, and never with a countdown clock.

---

## 8. Messaging pillars (with accuracy guardrails)

Anchor copy to verified facts; the racing superlatives are a minefield.

- ✅ "A block and a half from the **oldest organized major sporting venue in America**." 
- ✅ "The **oldest continuously operating thoroughbred track** in the country." 
- ❌ **Do not** write "the oldest racetrack in America." It's defensibly only ~**4th-oldest**
  by founding date (after Fair Grounds 1838, Freehold 1854, Pleasanton 1858). The whole
  competitive set fudges this; *our* accuracy can be a quiet flex.[^2][^12]
- ✅ "Organized in **1863** by John Morrissey; the Travers first run **1864**."[^3]
- ✅ SPAC / springs as *nearby* draws — attribute SPAC's scale to SPAC; don't invent
  numbers. The ~500,000 / $105M figures are SPAC's own commissioned study — fine to cite,
  not to launder as fact.[^6]
- ⚠️ Verify the **George Crippen / "Sunnyside"** house origin (single source) before it
  goes on the site.[^8]

---

## 9. Success criteria

The redesign is working when:
1. A first-time visitor understands within one screen that this is **a trackside Victorian
   house with a supper club**, not a downtown hotel.
2. The site reads as **calm and confident** — zero urgency-marketing tics.
3. Off-season pages give a non-racing guest a real reason to come (springs, romance, Libby,
   foliage, holidays).
4. The "living, breathing" motion is felt but never *costs* legibility or speed
   (reduced-motion respected, photography lazy, LCP fast).

---

## 10. Open questions to close next

- Documented architectural/ownership history of **55 Union Avenue** (confirm Crippen /
  "Sunnyside," build details, any historic-register status).
- Real **meet-season rate premium & occupancy** vs. off-season, to size the packages play.
- Quantified **visitor-segment mix** (racing vs. SPAC vs. wellness vs. weddings) and how
  booking lead-times differ by season.
- Libby Supper Club's own brand assets — is it co-branded with the inn, or a destination in
  its own right? (Affects nav prominence and whether it gets its own micro-site.)
